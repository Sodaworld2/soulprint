Art Forgery Case Studies

5

The cases that shaped modern authentication science. Each forgery teaches us what to detect.

Wolfgang Beltracchi — The Titanium White Particle

FORGERY CASE · 35 YEARS · CAUGHT 2010

Most successful modern forger. 35 years producing fake Max Ernst, Campendonk, Leger. Fabricated elaborate provenance including fake pre-war photographs. Caught because his "Zinc White" tube was contaminated with titanium dioxide — a pigment not available until after 1920, used in a painting attributed to 1914. ONE particle ended a career.

Lesson: Material anachronisms are the killer. Even the most meticulous forger leaves traces in the materials themselves.
Center for Art Law → Yale Scientific →

Han van Meegeren — The Vermeer Forger

FORGERY CASE · 6+ FAKE VERMEERS · 1930s-1940s

Produced fake "Vermeers" that fooled the entire Dutch art establishment. His "Christ at Emmaus" was declared "the masterpiece of Vermeer" by leading scholar Abraham Bredius. Used Bakelite (phenol formaldehyde) to artificially harden paint. Confessed to avoid treason charges for selling "national treasure" to Goering.

Lesson: The creator's process materials leave traces that outlast their intent to deceive. Bakelite is now a definitive van Meegeren attribution marker.
FutureLearn → Winterthur Museum →

Giovanni Morelli — The Original "Soulprint" (1891)

AUTHENTICATION METHOD · FOUNDATIONAL

Core insight: artists reveal themselves in details they do NOT consciously control. When painting ears, fingernails, drapery — artists fall into unconscious motor-memory shortcuts. A forger focuses on getting composition right; they rarely notice how Raphael's earlobes curl differently from Perugino's. Drew systematic comparison charts of ears, hands, noses across artists' oeuvres.

This IS Soulprint's philosophical foundation. The "soulprint" = the pattern of unconscious micro-decisions the artist doesn't know they're making.
Medium: Morelli → Fine Art Investigations →

FBI Art Crime Team

LAW ENFORCEMENT · EST. 2004 · $1B+ RECOVERED

Recovered 20,000+ items valued over $1 billion. Uses convergence model: subject matter experts + artist foundations + people who worked with the artist + scientific analysis + National Stolen Art File database. No single technique authenticates — convergence of independent signals is the standard.

Soulprint's 6-layer model is the digital equivalent of the FBI's convergence approach.

PHYSICS · UNIVERSITY OF OREGON

"Dimensional interplay fractal analysis" distinguished 14 authentic Pollock drip paintings from 51 non-Pollocks with 100% accuracy. Pollock's paintings are fractal, multifractal, and multiscaling — they evolved to higher complexity from 1945-1950. The fractal dimension is a measurable, distinctive signature.

Art Authentication Science

14

Museum lab techniques and their digital computational equivalents.

X-Ray Fluorescence (XRF) Spectroscopy

MUSEUM TECHNIQUE · NON-INVASIVE

Identifies elemental composition of pigments. Every pigment has a chemical signature tied to a historical period. Titanium white not available before ~1920. Zinc white from 1830s. Macro XRF scanning creates full elemental maps. Digital equivalent: color channel separation (RGB/HSV/LAB).

Bruker → ColourLex →

Infrared Reflectography (IRR)

MUSEUM TECHNIQUE · 900-1700nm

Near-infrared penetrates paint layers, reveals underdrawings. Pentimenti (artist's revisions during creation) = powerful authenticity signal. A copyist reproduces the final image; an original artist revises constantly. Digital equivalent: creation biometrics (undo/redo patterns).

IRInfo → ARTEnet →

Raking Light Analysis

MUSEUM TECHNIQUE · SURFACE TEXTURE

Light at narrow angle exaggerates surface texture. Reveals brushstroke relief, direction, length, pressure, speed. Like handwriting — unique to each artist, extremely difficult to fake. Digital equivalent: Sobel edge detection, gradient orientation histograms.

Cincinnati Art Museum → ARTEnet →

Canvas Thread Counting / Weave Analysis

MUSEUM TECHNIQUE · FOURIER ANALYSIS

X-ray images analysed for weave density using short-time Fourier analysis. Creates "weave maps." Canvases from the same bolt share identical stripe patterns ("rollmates"). Used to authenticate Vermeer. Digital equivalent: FFT frequency analysis.

IEEE: Thread Counting → arXiv: Fabric Comparison →

OCT Craquelure Analysis

MUSEUM TECHNIQUE · 7-8 MICROMETER RESOLUTION

Non-invasive cross-sectional imaging. Critical finding: authentic age cracks are rectangular in cross-section ("thin/deep" or "wide/shallow"). Forged cracks are triangular ("thin/shallow" or "wide/deep"). Craquelure patterns = fingerprints. Digital equivalent: fractal dimension analysis.

PLOS ONE → Nature: U-Net Craquelure →

Radiocarbon Dating (Bomb Peak)

MUSEUM TECHNIQUE · DEFINITIVE

Nuclear testing from 1954-1963 doubled atmospheric C-14. Fresh paint binder with post-1950 C-14 = modern forgery proven beyond doubt. Even forgers using old canvases are caught. AMS can date microgram-scale individual paint layers.

PNAS → Chemistry World →

Wavelet Decomposition — The PNAS Method

PNAS 2004 · DARTMOUTH · SEMINAL PAPER

Statistical models from authenticated works via wavelet coefficients at multiple scales. Tested on 13 Pieter Bruegel drawings — confirmed expert authentications. Identified number of contributing artists in Perugino painting, matching expert opinion. Applied to van Gogh, Gauguin, Giotto.

Multi-scale texture statistics capture brushstroke characteristics at every resolution — the most proven computational authentication method.
PNAS Paper → Northwestern →

AI-Generated Image Detection

10

State-of-the-art techniques to detect whether an image was made by a human or an AI model.

FIRE: Frequency-Guided Reconstruction Error

CVPR 2025 · OPEN SOURCE · MOST IMPORTANT

Diffusion models struggle to reconstruct mid-band frequency information in real images. Extract frequencies, run through diffusion model, compare reconstruction error. Real = high error. AI = low error. Generalises to unseen models. Robust against perturbations.

This is the primary AI detection engine for Soulprint. Open source and proven.
arXiv Paper → GitHub: FIRE →

ManiFPT: Generative Model Fingerprints

CVPR 2024 · MODEL ATTRIBUTION

Not just "was this AI?" but "WHICH AI model?" Defines the fingerprint of a generative model as the set of all its artifacts — the difference between a generated sample and its closest point on the real data manifold. Identifies Stable Diffusion vs DALL-E vs Midjourney.

Project Page → CVPR Paper (PDF) →

Fourier-Based GAN Fingerprint Detection

OCTOBER 2025 · >99% ACCURACY

2D DFT → log-transform magnitude spectrum → ResNet50. ALL GAN architectures leave spectral artifacts from upsampling. CycleGAN/StarGAN: dots and lines. StyleGAN: mid/high-frequency anomalies. Over 99% detection accuracy on standard benchmarks.

arXiv →

Diffusion Snap-Back Reconstruction

NOVEMBER 2025 · MANIFOLD DISTANCE

Add noise → denoise with diffusion model → compare reconstruction distance. Real images and generated images "snap back" to different manifold positions. The distance reveals origin. Conceptually elegant and hard to game.

arXiv →

GAN Spectral Artifacts via FFT

IEEE 2020 / arXiv 2020

Original discovery: ALL GANs leave frequency domain artifacts caused by upsampling operations (transposed convolutions, nearest-neighbor, bilinear). These create periodic patterns visible in the Fourier spectrum. Different architectures = different artifact signatures.

arXiv: Fake Painting Detection → IEEE →

Combined Uncertainty Measures for AI Detection

NATURE SCIENTIFIC REPORTS 2025

Fisher Information + Monte Carlo Dropout entropy + Deep Kernel Learning predictive variance + Particle Swarm Optimisation for adaptive thresholds. Multi-signal approach combining multiple uncertainty measures outperforms single-metric detectors.

Nature →

Error Level Analysis (ELA)

DIGITAL FORENSICS · BROWSER-NATIVE

Re-compress JPEG at target quality, compute per-pixel difference. Uniform = consistent compression history. Bright spots = different compression cycles = edits/pastes/AI generation. Runs in browser via Canvas toDataURL().

29a.ch: HTML5 ELA → FotoForensics Tutorial →

PRNU Camera Sensor Fingerprinting

IEEE 2006 · INDIVIDUAL DEVICE ID

Every sensor has unique noise from manufacturing (Photo Response Non-Uniformity). Present in EVERY image the sensor produces. Survives JPEG, filtering, processing. Identifies the specific physical camera — not just model, the individual unit. Used by FBI/law enforcement.

IEEE: PRNU → PMC: Smartphones →

STATISTICAL ANALYSIS · p < 0.0001

Grey-Level Co-occurrence Matrix extracts: Contrast, Energy, Entropy, Homogeneity. Real vs AI images show highly significant differences (t-statistic 24.50 for contrast, 20.02 for entropy). But newer models are closing this gap — needs multi-signal approach.

BECOMING ISO · ADOBE/GOOGLE/MS/LEICA

Open technical standard. Content Credentials = "nutrition label" for digital content — who made it, when, with what tools, every edit. RFC 3161 — Trusted Timestamps

IETF STANDARD · HSM-BACKED

Client computes hash → sends to TSA → TSA binds hash with time → signs with private key via HSM → returns token. Data never sent to TSA (only hash). Verification: compare hash, validate pair, verify signature chain. Legally admissible timestamp.

RFC 3161 → Metaspike: Digital Forensics →

ISO/IEC 27037 — Digital Evidence

INTERNATIONAL STANDARD

Four activities: Identification, Collection, Acquisition, Preservation. Evidence secured preventing spoliation/tampering. Complete documentation of every handler, transfer, access. "If you didn't write it down, it didn't happen." The chain-of-custody standard.
