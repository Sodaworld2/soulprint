A 12-year-old today has spent more cumulative hours in Roblox, Fortnite, and Minecraft than they have in any physical public space. Their identity — how they present themselves, how they're recognized by peers, their social status — is hard-wired into these virtual environments. Their avatar IS them. Their virtual home IS their space. The artifacts they've collected, built, and customized ARE their possessions.

This isn't a phase. This is permanent. When these kids are 25, their sense of ownership, identity, and value will be natively virtual.
